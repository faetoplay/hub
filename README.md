# FaetoPlay
A two-page static site: a landing page with my links, and a banner-by-banner TODO checklist for Infinity Nikki. No install, no account — just open it in a browser and check tasks off. The checklist covers one banner at a time and is rebuilt for each new one, so it stays short enough to actually finish.
**Live here:** https://faetoplay.com

## What's here
- **Landing page** — socials, Ko-fi, and links to my guides and tools.
- **Infinity Nikki Banner Checklist** — every task for the current banner, sorted by the last day you can safely start it.

## How to use
1. Open the checklist and read from the top. Tasks are grouped into **Start now**, **Start within 3 days**, and **Later in the banner**.
2. Work down the list. Tasks move up on their own as their deadlines approach, so anything urgent is always near the top.
3. Check tasks off as you go. Progress saves automatically in your browser.

A task with a **Locked** badge is waiting on something — a story chapter you haven't finished, or an event day that hasn't opened yet. Its checkbox stays disabled until then, and unlocks on its own once the thing it's waiting on is done.

Pinned at the very top are **reminders**: recurring things with no checkbox, because ticking the same box every day isn't useful.

Saved progress is **per-device** — it won't sync across devices.
> **Heads up:** progress is stored in your browser's `localStorage`, so it resets if that gets cleared. That can happen if you clear your browsing data / cookies / site data, use the page in a private/incognito window, or have a browser extension or setting that auto-clears site data. Normal refreshing and closing the tab are fine.

Checkmarks are also tied to the banner they belong to. When a new banner starts, the list resets to empty on purpose — last banner's progress isn't carried over.

## AI usage and reuse disclosure
AI was used to assist in building this site and the checklist. Every task on the list comes from in-game screenshots, but the timing behind it does not: the "start by" runway on each task is my own estimate of how long that task takes at a pace I'd call comfortable. That reflects how I play, which introduces a natural bias — a faster player will find some of it early, and a more casual one may find it tight. Treat the ordering as a suggestion, not a schedule. I am open to suggestions on changes, but note they may not be implemented.

Again: the intent is to keep it local, keep it simple, and give people one page to check instead of a dozen in-game menus. I will not be offering accounts, cross-device sync, or any kind of game integration. Anyone is free to use this checklist to build such functionality, I only request that you credit my contribution and that you disclose the AI usage.
